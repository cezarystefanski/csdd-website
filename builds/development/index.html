Design

Responsive Web Design

The mobile revolution is no longer an estimation or wishful thinking. Google announced that mobile searches in fact surpassed desktop searches. Their new searching algorithm is active - pages that adapt to mobile devices are now appearing higher in the search results. The BIA Kelsey report suggests that by 2018, mobile queries will be double that of the traditional ones. And that is not surprising. Chances are, you, the reader, is also reading this website on a smartphone. The worldwide accessibility of information on the go is perhaps one of the biggest breakthroughs of the modern internet technology.

However, while the biggest sites have adapted to the increased traffic through the use of phones and tablets, over 80% of the top 10,000 sites in the world are still not ready for this. That kind of behaviour results in what is called a site abandonment. A user seeing a slow loading, huge, hard to navigate on a small screen page (sometimes still with Flash elements) is far more likely to back out of it and just click another result. While there is no clear standard because of the ever-changing mobile device screen revolutions, responsive web design aims to make the website adapt, or 'respond' to those various sizes. This site is in fact responsive. If you are using a desktop browser, go ahead and resize it to a more mobile width. It's still readable and navigates quite nice on that width, wouldn't you say?

Good User Experience

A user on the web doesn't need to be attacked by content. He needs a legible, clean and intuitive experience while browsing. User Experience, or UX is one of the main goals of modern web design. And that a great thing. If a user comes across a complicated, non-readable website, he is far more likely to click out and navigate to another result. Obviously, you don't want this to happen. That's why a lot of contemporary apps and sites adopt a simple, fool-proof approach to their content. When you open up Facebook, you don't need to scroll through or wonder where to go next. The first things you see are bright red notification icons, and even before clicking, you just know by looking at them which one is messages and which is new friend requests. If you're using a phone, the icons are placed so you can reach them with your finger. That's how a great UX design works.

Development

Development is pretty much like modern magic. You do some strange things with some letters and numbers and magical things happen in a website. Look a few years back, when only static websites were around. There wasn't much in terms of interactivity, all you could do is click to load a new page with new content. Now, all you want to show, can be loaded dynamically and changed according to the user's behaviour. You can even load up your content in an external file and let the website parse and present it. There are literally thousands of different solutions and tools to make your website more magical each day, and it's my work to choose which ones apply best for your website and leave you clients amazed.
